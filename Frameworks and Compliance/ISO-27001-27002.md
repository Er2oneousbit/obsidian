# ISO 27001 / ISO 27002

#Compliance #ISO27001 #ISO27002 #ISMS #Frameworks

## What is this?

**ISO 27001** — International standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). Defines requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continually improving information security. **ISO 27002** provides detailed implementation guidance and the catalog of best-practice controls (non-mandatory code of practice). Current revisions: **ISO/IEC 27001:2022** and **ISO/IEC 27002:2022**.

---

## Overview

**ISO 27001 = Framework** (what you must do); **ISO 27002 = Guidance** (how to do it).

ISO 27001 is **process-focused**: requires you to identify risks, select controls, document decisions, and continuously improve. Not prescriptive (like PCI-DSS) but requires a management system.

**Certification:** Third-party audit (Certification Bodies like TÜV, BSI, etc.); valid 3 years with annual surveillance audits.

**Who needs it:** Global organizations, EU/UK enterprises, anyone with an "information security management system" requirement (increasingly common in RFPs).

---

## ISO 27001 Structure (Clauses 4–10)

The **mandatory management-system requirements** live in clauses 4–10 (clauses 1–3 are scope, references, and terms). These are what you're audited against; the Annex A controls (ISO 27002) support them.

### Clause 4: Context of the Organization
- **Internal/external context:** understand your business environment, threats, stakeholders.
- **Interested parties:** identify who cares about your information security (customers, employees, regulators).
- **ISMS scope:** define boundaries (which systems, locations, business units).

### Clause 5: Leadership & Commitment
- Top management must demonstrate commitment to the ISMS.
- Information security policy established and communicated.
- Roles & responsibilities assigned (CISO or equivalent).

### Clause 6: Planning
- **Risk assessment:** identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, impact.
- **Risk treatment:** decide to accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid each risk.
- **Statement of Applicability (SoA):** document which Annex A controls apply and why (mandatory).

### Clause 7: Support
- **Resources / competence / awareness:** budget, trained staff, security-awareness program.
- **Communication** and **documented information** (policies, procedures, evidence).

### Clause 8: Operation
- **Operational planning & control:** execute controls; monitor compliance.
- **Risk assessment/treatment:** ongoing; update as threats/business change.

### Clause 9: Performance Evaluation
- **Monitoring & measurement:** metrics for ISMS effectiveness.
- **Internal audits** and **management review**.

### Clause 10: Improvement
- **Nonconformities & corrective actions:** root-cause and fix control failures.
- **Continual improvement** of the ISMS.

## ISO 27002:2022 Controls (4 Themes)

ISO 27002:2022 reorganizes the Annex A controls into **4 themes** (down from the 2013 version's 14 domains). Contains **93 controls** total (vs. 114 in 2013), of which **11 are new**.

| Theme | Clause | Controls |
|---|---|---|
| Organizational | A.5 | 37 |
| People | A.6 | 8 |
| Physical | A.7 | 14 |
| Technological | A.8 | 34 |

> [!note]
> **Common trap:** 2022 has only these four themes — there is no "A.9" or "A.10" theme. Governance, supplier, incident, and asset controls all sit **inside A.5 Organizational**. Control numbers also shifted (e.g. "User endpoint devices" is **A.8.1** in 2022, not A.7.x).

### A.5 — Organizational Controls (37)

Policies, governance, and the broad management controls. Representative:
- **A.5.1** Policies for information security; **A.5.2** roles & responsibilities; **A.5.3** segregation of duties.
- **A.5.7** Threat intelligence *(new)*.
- **A.5.9–A.5.11** Asset management (inventory, acceptable use, return of assets); **A.5.12** information classification.
- **A.5.15–A.5.18** Access control, identity management, authentication information, access rights.
- **A.5.19–A.5.23** Supplier relationships, including **A.5.23** information security for use of cloud services *(new)*.
- **A.5.24–A.5.28** Information security incident management (planning, assessment, response, evidence).
- **A.5.29–A.5.30** Continuity, and **A.5.30** ICT readiness for business continuity *(new)*.
- **A.5.31** Legal, statutory, regulatory requirements; **A.5.35** independent review of information security.

### A.6 — People Controls (8)

- **A.6.1** Screening; **A.6.2** terms and conditions of employment.
- **A.6.3** Security awareness, education, and training; **A.6.4** disciplinary process.
- **A.6.5** Responsibilities after termination or change of role; **A.6.6** confidentiality/NDAs.
- **A.6.7** Remote working *(new)*; **A.6.8** information security event reporting.

### A.7 — Physical Controls (14)

- **A.7.1** Physical security perimeters; **A.7.2** physical entry; **A.7.3** securing offices, rooms, and facilities.
- **A.7.4** Physical security monitoring *(new)*; **A.7.5** protecting against physical and environmental threats.
- **A.7.6** Working in secure areas; **A.7.7** clear desk and clear screen.
- **A.7.8–A.7.10** Equipment siting/protection, assets off-premises, storage media.
- **A.7.11–A.7.14** Supporting utilities, cabling security, equipment maintenance, secure disposal/reuse.

### A.8 — Technological Controls (34)

- **A.8.1** User endpoint devices; **A.8.2** privileged access rights; **A.8.3** information access restriction; **A.8.5** secure authentication.
- **A.8.7** Protection against malware; **A.8.8** management of technical vulnerabilities (patch/vuln management).
- **A.8.9** Configuration management *(new)*; **A.8.10** information deletion *(new)*; **A.8.11** data masking *(new)*; **A.8.12** data leakage prevention *(new)*.
- **A.8.15** Logging; **A.8.16** monitoring activities *(new)*; **A.8.17** clock synchronization.
- **A.8.20–A.8.23** Networks security, security of network services, segregation, **A.8.23** web filtering *(new)*.
- **A.8.24** Use of cryptography; **A.8.25–A.8.28** secure development lifecycle, application security requirements, and **A.8.28** secure coding *(new)*.
- **A.8.13–A.8.14** Information backup and redundancy; **A.8.31** separation of dev/test/production environments.

> [!tip]
> The **11 new 2022 controls** are the most likely audit/pentest talking points: A.5.7 threat intelligence, A.5.23 cloud services, A.5.30 ICT continuity readiness, A.7.4 physical monitoring, A.8.9 configuration management, A.8.10 information deletion, A.8.11 data masking, A.8.12 DLP, A.8.16 monitoring, A.8.23 web filtering, A.8.28 secure coding.

## ISO 27001 Certification Process

### Phase 1: Planning (Months 1–2)
1. **Auditor selection**: Choose an accredited Certification Body (CB).
2. **Scope definition**: which systems/locations/data are in ISMS scope.
3. **Gap analysis**: compare current state to ISO 27001 requirements.
4. **Remediation plan**: identify and fix gaps.

### Phase 2: Gap Remediation (Months 3–9)
- Implement missing controls (access management, encryption, logging, etc.).
- Document policies, procedures, risk assessment, and the Statement of Applicability.
- Train staff; run an internal audit and fix findings.

### Phase 3: Stage 1 Audit (Month 10)
- Auditor reviews documentation; verifies the ISMS is designed to meet requirements.

### Phase 4: Stage 2 Audit (Months 11–12)
- On-site audit: auditor tests controls in operation (logs, interviews, sampled controls).

### Phase 5: Certification (Month 13)
- Certificate issued (valid 3 years); annual surveillance audits follow.

**Timeline**: 12–18 months total. **Cost**: $10K–$100K+ depending on org size.

## ISO 27001 vs. Other Frameworks

| Framework | Type | Scope | Rigor | Audit | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **ISO 27001** | Management System | Information security (broad) | High | 3-year cert + annual surveillance | $10K–$100K |
| **SOC 2 Type II** | Control Assessment | Security + optional criteria | Medium–High | ~1 year period | $5K–$50K |
| **HITRUST** | Compliance Standard | Healthcare security | Very High | 2-year cert | $5K–$100K |
| **NIST CSF** | Framework | Cybersecurity (broad) | Medium | Self-assessed | $0–$10K |
| **PCI-DSS** | Compliance Standard | Payment card data | High | Annual | $0–$20K |

See [[SOC-2-Type-II]], [[HITRUST]], [[NIST-CSF]], [[PCI-DSS-v4]].

**ISO 27001 Advantage**: Global recognition, process-focused (not just controls), flexible scope.

## Common ISO 27001 Implementation Mistakes

| Mistake | Impact | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No formal risk assessment | ISMS not grounded in actual risks | Conduct and document a risk assessment |
| Controls without justification | Auditor asks "why this control?" | Map controls to risks in the Statement of Applicability |
| Documentation not maintained | Auditor finds stale procedures | Assign a document owner; review annually |
| No management review | ISMS becomes stale | Senior management reviews the ISMS regularly |
| Auditor-only ISMS | Organization doesn't own it | Build the ISMS into day-to-day operations |
| No internal audits | Gaps surface only at certification | Annual internal audit; fix findings first |

> [!note]
> ISO 27001 is not a "pass once" — it's an ongoing management system that must be maintained through surveillance audits (years 1–2) and re-certification (year 3).
